About Lamborghini

Lamborghini, officially Automobili Lamborghini S.p.A., is a renowned Italian luxury sports car manufacturer founded by Ferruccio Lamborghini in 1963 in Sant'Agata Bolognese, Italy. Initially established to rival Ferrari, Lamborghini's journey began with the iconic 350 GT, which demonstrated exceptional engineering and luxury. The brand gained fame in the 1960s and 1970s with revolutionary designs like the Miura, credited as the world’s first mid-engine supercar. Despite financial challenges in the 1970s and 1980s, Lamborghini persisted, changing ownership multiple times before being acquired by Audi, a subsidiary of the Volkswagen Group, in 1998. Today, Lamborghini continues to set benchmarks in the automotive world with legendary models like the Aventador, Huracán, and more, blending cutting-edge technology with Italian craftsmanship.
